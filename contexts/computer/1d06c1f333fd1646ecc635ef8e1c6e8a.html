Hey folks, I have an H430 Desktop computer, and picked up a GeForce 8400GS video card (PCI express slot) so that I can run 2 VGA monitors for an "extended" desktop.
I keep having the monitors blink off, then back on, randomly. I'm trying to troubleshoot the problem, and the chime that is going off is the "disconnect" chime, but I can find no event (in event viewer) that corresponds to each of "blinks".
The maker of the Galaxy board wants to know the motherboard make/model, and the power supply specs. I see that the power supply max is 250W, and the video card says it needs at least 300W.
Do you think this is my problem?
R, Tom P

Expert's Answer

Welcome to Justanswer.

If your PSU (power supply unit) has only 250W it is definitely too weak to support the video card. You should get a PSU that features at least 400W or ideally 500W so all the PC components get enough power.

If you have a standard ATX format desktop computer, you can use most PSUs. I recommend using this one:

http://www.amazon.com/Cooler-Master-eXtreme-Supply-RS500-PCARD3-US/dp/B001G0WPLK/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1352208570&sr=8-1&keywords=psu+500w
