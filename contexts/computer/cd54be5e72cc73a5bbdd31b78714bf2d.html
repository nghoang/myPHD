Application Due: Open Until Filled
Type: Part-Time/Adjunct
This job supports the instructional component of Team Sill Leadership Academy. The Central Texas College instructor provides quality instruction and is committed to the personal development of each student.

Minimum Required Education:
  • Master's Degree in academic subject area with eighteen graduate semester hours in the teaching field.
  • Bachelor's Degree in technical/vocational subjects with three years of direct or related work experience in the subject area OR Associate's Degree in technical/vocational subjects with five years of direct or related work experience in the subject area.
Minimum Required Experience:
  • Experience in postsecondary teaching is preferred
Required Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:
  • Demonstrated knowledge of subject matter and
Essential Job Functions:
  • Prepare and present courses of instruction according to the Leader Skills Computer Program. Be thoroughly familiar with all Programs of Instruction.
  • Meet all regularly scheduled classes on time and for the full class period.
  • Demonstrate effective classroom management providing maximum opportunity for student progress.
  • Demonstrate organization of materials presented in the classroom
  • Be familiar with, and use, group instructional techniques and the unique challenges of computer instruction.
  • Utilize varied techniques of instruction.
  • Coordinate with the Site Director for the development and revision of course manuals to ensure that these materials reflect the latest doctrinal policies and procedures, academic and critical thinking and technical concepts. Report problems in resourcing as they occur.
  • Ensure proper management and utilization of all instructional equipment, resources, and materials.
  • Maintain accurate and complete records on each student.
  • Participate in orientation meetings, staff development workshops, and faculty meetings relevant to assigned duties.
  • Provide feedback to appropriate administrative staff for evaluation and revision of curriculum.
  • Ensure Army Education Center policies are followed. (No eating or smoking in the classrooms; no children in the classroom.)
  • Be supportive of college policies and philosophy.
  • Be familiar with specific procedures concerning proper registration, class rosters, certifications and evaluations for primary/standby students.
  • Provide class and other required documentation (such as personal point of contact information) to the Site Director in a timely manner.
  • Represent the institution in a positive manner as indicated by dress, decorum, and attitude.
  • Attend all scheduled pre-service and in-service training sessions.
  • Perform other duties as assigned by the Site Director.
NOTE: Management reserves the right to change these duties and responsibilities at any time.

Other Information:
  • Ability to communicate effectively and present materials clearly
