“Drones In A Contact Center”

Current Technical Support Representative in Tigard, OR – Reviewed 4 days ago – New

Pros – ACS provides new-hire and ongoing training. As one of the strongest business units of international giant Xerox, ACS has a huge list of Xerox employee perks and discounts. They promote from within a lot, and with locations all over the country (and worldwide), you can move sideways as well as upward.

Cons – Since most ACS jobs are in contact centers, the company is isolated from the rest of Xerox in terms of promotions and cross-moves. Pay ceiling is really low and even some members of middle management seem to not get paid as much as they should.

Advice to Senior Management – Pay your employees more, or make bonuses easier to achieve.

“Not going hungry...yet.”

Current Senior Management Consultant in Dallas, TX – Reviewed last week – New

Pros – 4 weeks vacation after 10 years service. Good manager. Good people to work with. A team atmosphere. Still working with a "Can do attitude". Training is available. Health benefits.

Cons – No raise in 5 years. Constantly dealing with changes that cause clients to lose productivity. No knowledge transition. Poor documentation. Constantly reinventing the wheel. Lack of automation due to old software and obsolete equipment due to Wallstreet expectations. We lose more money than we saved when equipment breaks down.

Advice to Senior Management – In order to be here in 10 years the accountants can't run the place with Wallstreet in mind. If you lose the techs that know the ins and outs of the datacenters and clients you will not be able to support the clients. The unseasoned off shore personnel can't compare to the talent that has been chopped from this organization. If the offshore personnel are good they move on to management or another company. Your personnel are valuable - quit treating them like just another number. We are the future of Xerox - look at the quarterly net profit - it is the same as ACS's quarterly profit before the merger less some from the Xerox drag on earnings from equipment sales.

“The perks fail to cover up the flaws.”

Former Customer Care Representative in North Bend, OR – Reviewed last week – New

Pros – Good benefits. Unlimited overtime. Almost always a full time position. Some places offer college credits for a local college. You usually have a team of other people going through the same thing as you to back you up. The supervisors are usually pretty good about helping you.

Cons – Mandatory overtime at least once a month. Very little break time, and short lunches. Goals purposely set to be unreachable. The calls aren't always bad, but the bad ones will cloud over any good ones. Nearly no chances for a promotion. Some management is brought in without any experience on taking calls. ACS is a vendor which means they care less about helping customers and more about the volume of calls they bring in, and everything they do revolves around it.

Advice to Senior Management – Take a month or two worth of calls before you dictate how a call flow should happen. Listen to the team supervisors. Stand up to the client now and then when they demand unreasonable goals.
