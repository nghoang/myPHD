Walgreens pharmacies suffer computer glitch

Walgreens pharmacies had a computer glitch on Tuesday. (Justin Sullivan/Getty Images / November 6, 2012)

By Peter Frost Tribune reporter

10:33 a.m. CST, November 6, 2012
Walgreen Co.'s pharmacies could not process insurance payments for about two hours on Tuesday morning because of a computer glitch, a spokesman said.
 
The Deerfield-based chain said the problem had been fixed by 8 a.m.
 
When the company's network falters, its pharmacy computers go into what's called "store-only mode." That allows its pharmacists to continue filling prescriptions for patients at the same price they paid the previous time the prescription was filled, said Michael Polzin, the spokesman.

Once the system is running again, Walgreen processes the prescription payment information and adjudicates any difference in payment.
