What is computer forensics investigation and why is computer forensics training so popular

Sponsored Links

Computer forensics refers to the investigation and analysis of the digital trail of a crime in the pursuit of evidence that can be used in a court of law On a humorous note, computer forensics training is popular since computer forensics enables computer geeks to  pretend to be expert policeman or spymasters.

In reality, computer forensics investigation is essentially a science and like any other scientific topic, computer forensics training is in high demand.

Stages involved in computer forensics that is typically covered in computer forensics training

  • Computer forensics training stage 1:

Safeguarding of all the digital evidence including keeping the digital evidence including PCs, floppy disks and other accessories under lock and key

  • Computer forensics training stage 2:

Finding and cataloguing  all files in the systems under investigation including all visible files,deleted files, encrypted files

  • Computer forensics training stage 3:

Data recovery of all hidden files, undelete of  files, decryption of encrypted files and cracking password-protected files

  • Computer forensics stage 4:

Data analysis of all digital evidence in relevance to the computer forensics investigation

  • Computer forensics stage 5:

Printing and creating presentable content in a court of law of all relevant data gathered through computer forensics investigation

  • Computer forensics stage 6:

Provide expert consultations to prosecutors and lawyers in a court of law.
