A bit of lore about the 6502 processor. The 6502 was based on the T6500 and T6501 processors designed at Motorola. As the story has it, the 6500 series was dropped in favor of the 6800 series and the designers on the T6500 and T6501 quit Motorola and founded MOS Technology to manufacture the 6501 processor. This violated certain contractual agreements ( Intellectual Property Ownership ) so some of the Motorla people help redesign the chip to avoid the bad PR of the Big Corporation sues plucks entrepreneur fiasco, while saving face for both Motorola and its former employees. Becasue of its low price, the 6502 was picked up by many hobbyists and pretty much kick-started the Home computer business. Eventually MOS Technology was bought by Commodore.
