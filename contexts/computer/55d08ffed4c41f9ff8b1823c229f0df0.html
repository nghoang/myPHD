Quote:
Originally Posted by Trixie
Wouldn't that be illegal?

I'm talking about getting this from the sites that have books and radio shows for listening that are in the public domain, and I'm assuming they have dealt with the question of copyright - I hope.
It would not necessarily be illegal and some do allow cd's do be burned. Just because something is "out there" to be listened to, whether on the radio or internet does not mean there is no copyright.

Truthfully, what is illegal is distributing or selling something without paying for the copyright or by removing the copyright. Certainly if something is protected from copying that you download and you remove that protection and THEN distribute it to people via internet (as in it is NOT just a copy that you keep for yourself to listen to in the car) that would be highly illegal.

To answer your question with good instructions though, we'd have to know where you intend to get these audio books from before we could research the easiest way for you (if it's even possible) to burn them to a disk.

There actually are places that will furnish you with instructions that you can download audio books from and sometimes, not always, the process differs depending on where you get them from. So does the typical legality of it.

Copy protection is different than copyright, honestly. Making a backup for personal use is different than distributing bootlegs.
