Medic One, fire district to share space For the DAILY RECORD The Daily Record | 3 comments

Kittitas County Hospital District No. 2 has entered into a two-year agreement allowing an ambulance crewÊ to occupy Kittitas County Fire District No. 7Õs Bullfrog Road fire station 12 hours a day, according to a news release issued Tuesday by the hospital district.

For a fee, the agreement allows an ambulance crew to occupy Fire District No. 7Õs Station 76 from 8 a.m. until 8 p.m. every day. Ê


Hospital District No. 2 operates Medic One, the only advanced life support ambulance in Upper Kittitas County, according to the release. Medic One usually has two crews on duty during daytime hours and one crew on duty during night and evening hours, and the new agreement allows the two daytime crews to dispatch from different locations, with the goal of improving response times. The arrangement will delay the need for the Hospital District to build a new bay for its Medic One ambulance and allow volunteer District No. 7 firefighters to use the ambulance when Medic One crews are not on duty.

ÒThis agreement is an excellent example of how two separate agencies can work together to maximize the value of our residentsÕ tax dollars,Ó Kittitas County Fire District No. 7 Chairman Bob Cernick said in the release. ÒThe board and staff of Kittitas County Fire District No. 7 look forward to working with the commissioners and administration of Kittitas County Hospital District No. 2 and all Upper County emergency medical services providers to further enhance response times and services.Ó
