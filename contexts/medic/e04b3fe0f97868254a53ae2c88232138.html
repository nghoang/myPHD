[edit] Description

The Medic is a Terran unit produced from the Barracks (given an Academy is on the field) which starts with 50 energy (max 200 initially, max 250 with upgrade) and has no attack ability; its primary use is replenishing the HP of nearby biological units at the cost of its energy (2HP per 1 Energy). Medics can only heal one unit at a time, and multiple Medics cannot heal the same unit.

Terran can research Restoration, which Medics can use to remove the effects of spells such as Plague and Lockdown on a single unit, and Optic Flare, which can be used to reduce a unit's sight range to 1 and deactivate its detection ability, although this is almost never used. In rare instances, you can use optical flare against a unit that has been parasited, completely nullifying the parasite effect.

[edit] Abilities

Heal
Energy: 1 per 2 HP healed Range: 2
Effect-Radius: - Duration: -
Automatically (or on order) heals a close-by friendly biological ground unit.


Restoration
Energy: 50 Range: 6
Effect-Radius: - Duration: -
Removes Lockdown, Optic Flare, Irradiate, Plague, Ensnare, Parasite, and Maelstrom from targeted unit. (Cannot target units that are under the effect of Stasis Field and Defensive Matrix.)


Optic Flare
Energy: 75 Range: 9
Effect-Radius: - Duration: Infinite (can be removed by Restoration)
Reduces sight range of the targeted unit to 1, and removes its detection ability. A green "Blind" text will appear in the unit information box.

[edit] Competitive Usage

Marines are almost always used in combination with Medics, as Medics can replenish Marine health lost from using Stim Packs in addition to the health lost from enemy units' attacks. Proper micro and control group mix of Medics is crucial to the old strategy of MM.


[edit] versus Zerg

Medics are absolutely essential against the Zerg unless the Terran is using a Mech TvZ strategy. Medics essentially nullify the cost of using Stim, allowing Marines to deal more damage with less drawback. Without Medics, Marines are easily destroyed. If the Zerg is using Mutalisks, Marines and Medics are difficult to destroy without eliminating each unit in one hit. The Medic can also use restoration to negate the effect of the Defiler's plague on more expensive units. Tip : When you don't have Restoration, and don't want to waste energy by constantly healing plagued Marines, you can press the Move button and click on a Marine. This will prevent healing until you hit the stop / hold / attack / heal button.

[edit] versus Terran

Medics are generally not used against Terrans at the competitive level; Marines are not used often enough in this matchup to warrant the use of medics to heal them. Optic Flare can easily be countered with floating buildings to extend sight for units, and Restoration is unnecessary in this matchup (although UpMagic used it once to un-lockdown his Battlecruisers).

[edit] versus Protoss

Medics may be used against the Protoss in a quick M&M rush, though it is a very infrequently used strategy due to the difficulty of successful execution. However, they play a role in Deep Six build. One of the potential problems encountered by Marines and Medics is that both Reavers and High Templar are known for taking down large numbers of Marines and Medics.

Medics may be produced to use optic flare on Observers, preventing Dragoons from freely destroying Spider Mines although the overall effectiveness of this strategy is debated[1].

In one example game, Optic flare was used on Observers to great effect: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95qmgMA5JdE (Upmagic Vs Jangbi, Winners league 2-10-2010)

[edit] Medic Walls

Medics can be used as walling units to prevent melee units from reaching the Terran Marines or Siege Tanks. Enemy units that are attack-moving towards the Terran army will automatically try to attack the latter units, ignoring the Medics. This mechanic sees Medics blocking ramps and choke points to prevent Ultralisks and Zerglings from attacking the army.
