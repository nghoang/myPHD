About MEDIC EMS

   MEDIC EMS: Description of Our Service and Scope 

 
MEDIC EMS was founded in 1982 as a paramedic ambulance service by St. Luke’s and Mercy, two local Midwestern Davenport, Iowa hospitals.  MEDIC EMS began operations at the former St. Luke's Hospital (now Genesis Medical Center’s West campus) where crews operated out of the hospital’s garage. In 1986 Davenport Osteopathic (now known as Trinity Medical Center, Bettendorf, Iowa) became part of the corporation, and in 1998, public involvement was added when representatives from Scott County and the cities of Davenport and Bettendorf joined the Board of Directors. MEDIC EMS has since grown to 150 employees serving in four divisions, which include the Paramedic Division, the Transportation Division, the Eldridge and LeClaire Alternative Delivery Models, and MED-COM. With an annual operating budget of $8 million, MEDIC EMS covers a primary service area of approximately 450 square miles.
With a service area of over 300,000 residents, MEDIC EMS responds to 70 calls each day, totaling 27,000 calls in 2010. Our Paramedic Ambulance Service operates with 18 ambulances and four supervisor/disaster vehicles, working 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Over 60 Paramedics and 25 EMTs make up the two-person crews, which respond to calls at a Paramedic Specialist level. MEDIC EMS received a 5-year accreditation from the Commission on Accreditation of Ambulance Services (CAAS) for its compliance with national standards of excellence in 1996, with reaccreditations in 2001, 2004, 2007 and 2010.  Although accreditation is a common practice in many disciplines, it is very new to the ambulance service industry. MEDIC EMS was the 52nd ambulance service in the country to successfully complete the voluntary review process, which included completion of a comprehensive application and on-site review by national experts in the field of emergency medical services (EMS). With this achievement, MEDIC EMS became the first CAAS accredited ambulance service to be accredited in the States of Iowa and Illinois. MEDIC EMS began Critical Care Transports in 2006 to respond to the needs of the community for a higher level of ambulance transport. Critical Care Ambulance transport enables facilities to confidently transport high-acuity level patients without depleting their own internal clinical resources. MEDIC EMS instituted a bariatric cot and winch system in 2009, to make transporting very large patients safer. Our newest clinical improvement is the 2010 initiation of field Induced Hypothermia treatment for cardiac arrest patients, an intervention intended to improve not only survival, but the quality of life of these patients. 
 To respond to the growing needs of our rural communities, MEDIC EMS continues to explore viable options that provide the availability of effective emergency medical care. In 1999 MEDIC EMS built the Eldridge Alternative Delivery Model (ADM) in Eldridge, Iowa and in January of 2001 began an identical operation in LeClaire ADM in LeClaire, Iowa. The ADM pairs an in-house full-time paramedic with a local volunteer to provide the rural communities with high quality, round the clock Paramedic Specialist ambulance service in those service areas.
 
Responsible for tracking emergency calls, scheduled transfers, and all vehicle activity, our MED-COM division plays a central role in the delivery of quality patient care by MEDIC EMS in the Quad City area. Beginning operations on June 19, 1995, MED-COM operates 24-hours daily, receiving calls and dispatching vehicles. MED-COM receives approximately 40,000 requests for ambulance service annually, making logistical control essential.  MED-COM operates under the control of System Status Controllers (SSCs), who are specially trained medical dispatchers. MED-COM implemented Emergency Medical Dispatching (EMD) in 1998, providing this lifesaving service to callers in the cities of Bettendorf and Davenport, Iowa; in April, 2011, MED-COM became the designated EMD provider for all of Scott County, Iowa.  MED-COM dispatches ambulances for Genesis Medical Center-Illini Ambulance based in Silvis, Illinois, and gives pre-arrival instructions to the Illinois communities of Silvis, East Moline, and Northern Rock Island County in Illinois. MED-COM also provides local assistance for MED-FORCE Helicopter in Scott County, relaying requests for scene flights to the primary aeromedical dispatch agency. All SSCs maintain National Academy of Emergency Dispatch EMD certification, enabling them to provide concise, up-to-date, and state-of-the art pre-arrival instructions to all 9-1-1 callers. A Computer Aided Dispatch program (CAD) aids the SSCs in tracking ambulances at all times, enabling them to send the closest ambulance to an emergency call.    We have embraced Emergency Medical Dispatch since 1998, and believe that ACE accreditation should be the benchmark standard of practice for all dispatch agencies. As a CAAS accredited agency since 1996, we are committed to now seek ACE accreditation, validating that we meet or exceed the standards set forth by the National Academy of Emergency Dispatch. 
  
Community service is a large part of what we do at MEDIC EMS.
 
·         Mikey Medic program – A community school education program that in 2010 visited 20 schools and talked with over 2,000 children about calling 9-1-1 and what to do in emergencies.
·         Eldridge ADM Permanent Car Seat Fit Station – Since June of 1999, MEDIC EMS has held a monthly free car seat check. Over 500 Child Safety Seats have been checked and any defective or recalled seats have been replaced at no charge.
·         Bike Medic – This program is a volunteer program of MEDIC EMS employees. Funded by MEDIC EMS, volunteer paramedics ride Davenport’s bike paths and administer emergency medical care to anyone sick or injured. The Bike Medics are a great addition to large event coverage. They can respond through large crowds quickly.
·         LeClaire CPR Saturday - MEDIC EMS holds a community CPR class once per month in our LeClaire ADM station.
·         Senior Voice Committee (A city of Davenport committee) - MEDIC EMS has been on the Board of Directors of the Senior Voice Committee since its inception in 1999. MEDIC EMS has been heavily involved in the File of Life program. Over 24,000 seniors in Scott County have received a file of life to enhance their medical treatment.
·         Community Disaster Planning and Disaster Drills
 
MEDIC EMS is strongly committed to the evolving needs of the community. We provide many programs that enhance the quality of life in the Eastern Iowa/ Western Illinois area, and believe in delivering high quality, high value services to customers within our service area. 
Looking to the future, MEDIC EMS will move MED-COM into the Scott Emergency Communications Center in 2011 to co-locate with the other PSAPs of Scott County.
