Established in 1990, MEDiC is a student-run program of the University of Wisconsin School of Medicine and Public Health and is comprised of seven health clinics throughout Madison.

 

MEDiC's goals are twofold: first, to improve the health of the underserved in the Madison area and second, to educate University of Wisconsin-Madison health professions students.

Honoring MEDiC's 20th Anniversary

 

In celebration of its 20th anniversary of promoting the Wisconsin Idea and providing health care to the underserved, MEDiC has launched a $20 for 20 campaign.

 

For many students, MEDiC has provided a valuable opportunity to connect with the community. Watch the video to learn how MEDiC has been a cornerstone of Megan Schultz's education. Make a Gift Today 

20th Anniversary Event Photos

 

On October 28, MEDiC celebrated its 20th anniversary with students, physicians, community partners and UW faculty and staff. Speakers included MEDiC President Parker Hoerz; Drs. Goodfriend and Katcher, MEDiC founders; Dean Robert Golden; Pam Brunk of Salvation Army; Jill Watson of UW Foundation; and Jenny Ciske, MEDiC PR Coordinator. MEDiC was presented with the Presidential Service Award from President Obama. This event was made possible through the generous support of UW Health and the Wisconsin Medical Alumni Association. Embracing the Wisconsin Idea

 

MEDiC integrates students from the health sciences schools and programs across UW-Madison. They work together with health professionals to provide health care to those who who currently have no access, thus exemplifying the spirit of the Wisconsin Idea.
