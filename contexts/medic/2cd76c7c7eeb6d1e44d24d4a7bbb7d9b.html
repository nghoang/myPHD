In papers October 25

The Australian Defence Force recruits via augmented reality campaign.

The objective of this campaign is to recruit medical students worthy of a Defence Force University Sponsorship by testing their skills in a way that would simply not be possible in a traditional poster or even on-screen.

Through augmented reality app, medical students can diagnose and treat a series of patients on advertisements using true-to-life diagnostic techniques. Technology enables students to become completely immersed in the role of Medical Officer, letting them use CT scanning, x-ray, angiogram, stethoscope, ECG, and ultrasound on real and dramatic Defence Force scenarios.

With the app being the entrance exam, students who performed the best were phoned straight away to be congratulated, and offered their Defence University Scholarship.

This campaign has been in market for a few months, winning a slew of awards including  four Gold Lions at Cannes and the Quinlivan Black Award at The Caxtons. 

This double-page newspaper execution ran in a Brisbane commuter newspaper, targeting a younger demographic. The first page of the advertising feature explains the benefits of becoming a medical officer for the Defence Force, while the second page invites readers to diagnose the officer in the visual.
