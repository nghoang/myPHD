  • Review from William S.

    • 22 friends

    • William S.

    Chicago, IL

    7/22/2010

    I really wanted to put more stars on this review.

    I dealt with Powerbook Medic for work, through a glowing professional recommendation referencing the polite staff, swift and polished service, and quality hardware. This hasn't been entirely untrue.

    I work at an IT services firm, and our work sometimes includes hardware repair. Working on Apple hardware can be specialized work--it's hard to get the parts (and harder to get them for a decent price), the staff that does the work is twice as expensive, and the customers tend to be more demanding. We'd decided it'd be easier to offload this work to a firm dedicated to it.

    I spoke first to Sean (Shawn?), and later Kyle, both of whom were warm, charming, helpful people who seemed very capable but weren't in the decision-making position to set up a business-to-business relationship with me. The reason I spoke to both of them (and someone else in another department) so many times before I got to enter into this dialogue is because I had to leave at least three messages before I got in touch with Justin, the Operations Manager.

    Justin seemed baffled that I'd want to establish a personal relationship with someone at Powerbook Medic for my professional clients' very expensive equipment containing their very valuable data instead of going through the website, a stance he repeated to me twice before getting the hint that I'd like my hand held just a little bit. I had to call back twice before he remembered to set up our dealer account Ê(and before he remembered who I was) and send the instructions so that I could copy them to my staff. By the point we were good to go, we'd had to do the work on four of the Macbooks we'd had pending ourselves.

    Since we were set to go and didn't have a ready source for the parts the last machine needed, I decided to have faith in my recommendation and take the plunge, setting up a 2-way, overnight-shipped, expedited ticket for the repair.

    I was explicit that this was an audition for our Apple business, which gets not-insignificant traffic.

    It was explained to me that I'd get a call when the box arrived, where I'd be informed of the diagnostic results and be allowed to confirm the work to be done and payment.

    The good news? They work fast. I mean FAST, doing excellent work, with gorgeous parts.

    The bad news? I didn't hear about it until the package was already in transit, on its way back to the office. They forgot to call at all--I got a paid invoice in my email, and guessed something was wrong. It was explained to me that I tacitly waived my confirmation call by writing "expedite" on the box--which I'd done on the phone rep's advice.

    Because we didn't get the confirmation of work, we didn't have the opportunity to confirm the work with our client, nor to transfer money into the expense account that the debit card we'd given them was tied to. We incurred a nice, fat, $35 overdraft fee.

    Who's the only person who could do anything about this? Justin. Who decided to use poor, awkward Kyle as an intermediary to offer me a $22 credit for a $35 mistake? Justin. Who didn't care when I wrote a message explaining (and proving) the total of our fees and our discomfort with dealing with a company that doesn't do what it says, the way that it says it will? Justin.

    As I said in my message to Powerbook Medic, a $13 loss won't sink my company. What could sink us is if their mistake had been more sensitive. What if this had involved a data purge, or if our client's check had bounced or they'd refused payment? What if this laptop were involved in a lawsuit that the client suddenly discovered would be invalidated if the laptop were repaired?

    All of these things are things that have happened to me in my work--near-disasters that were averted only by our best-practices based habit of confirming work with the client before executing it. I'm not even mentioning that their action forced us to violate our policy of checking in with the client before the work is done. Good thing it was good work.

    This had the potential to be a lot more than a $13 mistake, and honestly, the attitude toward it transformed what should've been a minor error into a stinging indictment of Powerbook Medic management's attitude toward customer service, communication, and anything beyond the immediate bottom line. Time to reevaluate, guys.

    We'll give Powerbook Medic another shot sometime in the future, with a low-risk job, because again: great prices, great parts, great work, great repair staff. They've lost the 5-15 monthly Apple hardware jobs we were going to send them for now, however, which adds expense we have to pass to clients, but--better expensive and well done than cheap, unpredictable, and rude.

    The problems here rest squarely with management. ÊI hope I'll be able to update this with a better experience in the future.

  • Review from Tom V.

    • 2 friends
    • 1 review
    • Tom V.

    WOODLAND HLS, CA

    2/13/2012

    Short version: avoid this place like the plague!

    I have a unibody 17" Macbook Pro, whose screen was damaged. Based on a recommendation by a co-worker (who has apologized profusely for recommending this place to me), I sent it to Powerbook Medic, thinking they would turn it around in a week.

    Big mistake.

    About a week later, they called and told me that they had diagnosed my machine and their determination was that the LCD needed to be replaced. No duh. However, because it was the matte version, not glossy, and the bezel was damaged, I would have to wait for a bezel to become available from another machine, as Apple doesn't sell the bezel as a separate part. I told them to hold off, thinking it would only be a couple of weeks. However, after waiting, I decided that I really needed my laptop back and told them to go ahead with the repair and forget about the bezel (I found some on eBay and figured I would do that part of it myself). I waited...and waited...

    A month later, during the week between Christmas and New Year, I called them to find out what was taking so long. The tech that answered the phone told me that the person 'assigned' to my case was unavailable and asked if he could help. I had found that Apple will sell the whole lid with the LCD, bezel, camera, and everything as a complete part, so I asked for a price on doing that instead. The tech told me he would get a price and call me right back.

    Three days later, I hadn't heard anything from them so I called again. They did have a price on the whole display - $900. I told them to go ahead, since work was going to pay for the damage to my machine.

    A few weeks later, I still hadn't heard anything, so I called again. Once again, the tech 'assigned' to my machine was not available, but I was told that not only was the display bad, but I would need a new logic board, because the backlight wasn't working. This seemed odd to me, because when I shipped the machine, even though the screen had a gouge in it, the backlight still worked flawlessly, and had for a week following the damage. I asked for a price on a new logic board, and they told me they would have to get back to me - AGAIN.

    They actually called me back two days later and told me the bad news - a new logic board was $1200. Along with a new display, this would be $2100 in repairs. I told them that it was pointless as I could get a whole new laptop for that. I declined the whole repair, and told them to put the bad display back in and ship it back to me. To their credit, they didn't charge me a dime (originally, they wanted to charge return shipping but I refused to pay for it).

    The return shipping took just over a week, and when I opened the box, not only had they not re-installed the screen but had lost all the mounting screws for it. I emailed them and they told me that the screws were missing when I shipped the machine to them. I told them that unless Apple forgot to install the screws at the factory, that they were dead wrong. A few days later, I got an email with an apology and a promise to have the missing screws shipped back. I still have not received them.

    The same day I got my laptop back, I turned around and shipped it to DT&T Repair in Fresno. They turned my machine around in a week, installed a new display, free return shipping - for $590, all in. Best of all - there was nothing wrong with my logic board, as I suspected.

    I feel a bit bad writing a review like this - the people at Powerbook Medic were polite and well-meaning, but so hopelessly incompetent and disorganized, that I could not recommend them to anyone. I have been without my laptop for almost 3 months, which would be an eternity for most people. Luckily for me I removed the hard drive ahead of time and was able to use it in an external FW enclosure with another CPU until now.
