West Michigan Medic Crews Deployed to East Coast

Medic crews could spend 30 days helping after Hurricane Sandy hits

  • Comments
    0
Nicole DiDonato FOX 17 Morning Reporter

9:32 a.m. EDT, October 29, 2012
MUKSEGON, Mich.-—

As the East Coast braces for Hurricane Sandy, West Michigan is ready to help.

Promed Ambulance in Muskegon sent six medic crews to New Jersey. They left early Sunday morning and arrived in Fort Dix. From there, the crews headed to Manhattan.

  • Topics
  • Natural Disasters
  • Hurricanes

President Tom Schmiedeknecht said they are part of a deployment list that responds to disasters. The crews will likely transport patients from one hospital to another. It's similar to what they did during Hurricane Katrina, the last time their crews were deployed.

Schmiedeknecht says he expects the crews to be there for about 30 days.
