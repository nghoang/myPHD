1.0 out of 5 stars Try it, but forget about returning it July 18, 2012
By T. Goldfarb
Amazon Verified Purchase
I ordered this inflatable back pillow even though the price was higher than other products. When it arrived, the package instructions mentioned that the pillow worked best when it wasn't fully inflated. So naturally, I inflated it partially to try it out. No matter how much or little I inflated it, I couldn't get it to support my back comfortably.

The receipt said to call their phone center to get approval for any returns. When I called to request a refund, the representative I spoke with started by asking a question: "Did you try the pillow?" When I said yes, she then asked, "Did you blow it up?" Of course, I said yes. She replied: "Since you inflated it, we can't take it back." WHAT????

There was no warning ANYWHERE saying that inflating it would cancel the ability to return it. If that is their policy, there should have been a big notice inside the package telling buyers "IF YOU TRY THIS PILLOW, YOU WON'T BE ABLE TO RETURN IT." How ridiculous is that??

I wrote a complaint via Amazon's satisfaction guarantee, and Amazon forwarded it to the seller, who said:
>What you were told by our customer service rep was correct. We generally
>do not allow returns on this item, because the manufacturer does not
>accept returns on this item after they have been inflated, for obvious
>health and sanitation reasons. This used to be stated on our Amazon
>page, but for some reason, it currently does not appear, even though it
>still shows up on the page where product information is managed.
>
>That being the case, we will accept your return on this item, under our
>usually terms of return. Which means that you will be responsible for
>the cost of the return. You will receive a full refund on the product,
>but there not be a refund on the original shipping charge.

So, be warned! Don't order this product unless you're prepared to keep it, even it you don't like it!
--Toni Goldfarb
