MOUNTAIN medic 2

This course has been developed by our very own mountain expert Mr Ty Wood, to provide training for emergency situations, which may occur whilst mountain walking/hiking. This is recommended not just for Mountain group leaders, but for anyone taking part in this sport.

The course is taught over two days at our base camp style training facility in the East Sussex country side. Where we have an extensive facility and the option of camping on site and of course, use of the South Downs. Or alternatively we can come to you, depending on distance and group size. It is a totally practically based course with as little theory as possible, we find that students take in far more from hands on learning. Which means, THERE ARE NO POWERPOINT PRESENTATIONS!

This course covers all the requirements of the:

HSE Emergency First Aid certificate.

The course includes instruction in:

HSE approved first aid training
Mountain Rescue techniques and Group Leadership
Environment and activity specific injuries

Information
Lunch & refreshments are supplied throughout the day. (special dietary requirements,you are required to bring with you)

Instead of spending a boring day in a classroom why not spend it in the great outdoors being taught by some of the best and most experienced instructors in the field.

Not only will gain an HSE first aid qualification, you will have a great day out in the county side of East Sussex learning a lot more than basic first aid, all this for the same as you would pay to sit in a classroom .

First Aid content:
? Basic life support

? Vital signs Ð conscious level, breathing, pulse, temperature,colour

Ê Emergency action Ð a methodical system to manage emergencies

? Airway problems Ð recognising & managing airway problems

? Breathing problems Ð coping with choking

? Circulation problems Ð shock, chest pain,collapsing,heart stoppage

? Unconsciousness Ð collapsing, what can it be? what do you do?

? Bleeding Ð treating bleeding from open wounds

? Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) - UK Resuscitation Guidelines

? Fractures Ð splinting & dressing

? Head & spinal injuries Ð Cervical collars & dressing

? Environmental illnesses Ð Hypothermia & Hyperthermia

Survival/ Camp craft & Rescue Content

Shelter building - Construction of various types of shelter for use in emergency situations.

Fire starting - Collecting materials & the use of sources of ignition.

Open fire Cooking - Different methods over open fires.

Transportation of casualties - Rescue techniques & use of rescue equipment.

Navigation- We will teach you how to navigate with limited resources and under pressures. Just like in a real situation.
