Where are Medic-CE.com Continuing Education Units (CEUs) Accepted?

Continuing education requirements vary widely by state and are constantly changing. This document attempts to summarize state online continuing education policies.

The following information was compiled from December 2009 to January 2010 by browsing state EMS websites, as well as by directly calling state EMS certification offices. While a great deal of time was spent in an attempt to provide you with correct and current information, Medic-CE.com does not assume any liability for a person who takes a course or courses which is/are not accepted in that person’s particular state. Accordingly, it is the user’s responsibility to verify the accuracy of the information provided. State contact information and reference links are listed for users who seek further information.

Online Continuing Education Policy:

NREMT continuing education units can be earned using Medic-CE.com, as Medic-CE.com is a CECBEMS approved provider.

NREMT-First Responder:
  • Section 1: A maximum number of 6 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Reference Link: http://www.nremt.org/nremt/downloads/First.Responder.pdf

NREMT-Basic:
  • Section 1: A maximum number of 10 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Section 2: A maximum number of 24 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Reference Link: http://www.nremt.org/nremt/downloads/EMT.basic.pdf

NREMT-Intermediate:
  • Section 1: A maximum number of 10 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Section 2: A maximum number of 18 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Reference Links:
    http://www.nremt.org/nremt/downloads/EMT.Int85.pdf
    http://www.nremt.org/nremt/downloads/EMT.Int99.pdf

NREMT-Paramedic:
  • Section 1: A maximum number of 10 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Section 2: A maximum number of 12 hours can be applied from Distributive Education towards this section and must be state or CECBEMS approved.
  • Reference Link: http://www.nremt.org/nremt/downloads/EMT.Paramedic.pdf

Online Continuing Education Policy:

  • California continuing education units can be earned using Medic-CE.com, as Medic-CE.com is a CECBEMS approved provider.
  • The Medic-CE CECBEMS approval number is MedicCE6500.
  • Medic-CE.com provides access to an instructor 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, via email or telephone to answer any questions a student may have and is, therefore, classified as instructor-based continuing education.
  • California only awards credit hours above 1 hour in half hour increments. Therefore, any Medic-CE course that is 1.25 hours will count as 1 hour in the state of California, and any course that is 1.75 hours will count as 1.5 hours in the state of California. Consult your regional EMS office for further information.
