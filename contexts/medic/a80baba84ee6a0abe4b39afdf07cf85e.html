    DUBDUBDUB,

    If you want to find out about what the NREMT is doing, I suggest you contact them directly. If they don't have any knowledge of an overseas program, you risk spending money on something that won't be recognized when the time comes to submit for reciprocity.

    Apart from US military bases, I am not aware of any overseas NREMT programs. Last year, NREMT dictated that all paramedic programs must be affiliated with a recognized US education council in order to be recognized; this was to cut down on the multiple fly-by-night diploma mills that were having their buddies proctor the exams and letting students who shouldn't have passed, pass.

    NREMT - National Registry of Emergency Medical Technicians

    Hey Dub,
    The paramedic course here in Ireland gives more classroom hours than the NREMT Paramedic. The problem lies with the skills. The PHECC Paramedic is not an ALS Paramedic. If you choose to apply for reciprocity you will need to get those additional skills that PHECC doesn't include in your paramedic training.
    We offer cannulation and intubation skills down here in County Wexford if you want to expand your training. We do have a few PHECC Paramedics coming through our courses.

    Cheers
