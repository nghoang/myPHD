    REALLY GLAD I WENT WITH THE BIKE MEDIC INSTEAD OF GOING TO A SHOP.

    He came out to see me on a Sunday morning (about two weeks back), instead of me having to drive downtown to drop my bike off somewhere. His rates for a tuneup were cheaper than what others were charging, AND I didn't have to go anywhere? I was sold right there.

    Over the phone I explained what I thought was wrong with my bike and what I thought it needed, but because I wasn't too familiar with bike terminology I couldn't be sure I was explaining myself properly. When he showed up I could actually show him what I meant and he was able to educate me on bikes in general as well as help me figure out what I am really looking for in a bike!

    He was able to tell me what it was designed for (which put my experience with it in perspective), what I could expect to get out of that design, and what I could expect out of the average Walmart mountain bike.

    I'd say the education lesson was the best part of the service, because I was interested and lacking in that area. I even showed him another bike I had to get a "diagnostic" and he was honest enough to tell me that my money would be better spent buying a cheap new bike and getting it properly adjusted for about $15, rather than spending $90-120 in replacement parts and a tuneup.

    OH! And he discounted me on the tuneup, because I wouldn't have gotten the full value out of it based on the condition of the bike.

    Definitely recommending him to others and will be calling him up when I upgrade to a better bike.

About This Business

Provided by business

Specialties

Our new  Bike Shop in El Dorado Hills is now open!

Now in stock  KHS, Free Agent and Manhattan Bicycles....

All new bikes come with 30 day tune up, and lifetime adjustments.

Flat repairs start at $7.50 (includes tube and labor)

A tune up is only $60, includes:
Thorough cleaning, and frame polish, front and rear fine gear adjustment, front and rear brake adjustment,  tire pressure adjustment, front and rear wheel hub adjustment, bottom bracket and crank adjustment, headset adjustment, truing (straightening) your wheels, and torque all bolts. Many shops charge more, for a less-inclusive tune-up, averaging $75 to $100 just to cover the basics.

We offer FREE pick up and delivery for services over $50 within El Dorado Hills and Folsom.

History

Established in 2011

Meet the Business Owner: Casey M.

Cycling has always been a huge part of my life. I grew up racing bmx, and later mountain bikes with some big name supporters. I started wrenching and selling bikes in southern CA right around age 14, and even found time to dabble with wrenching in bike shops during my 10 year tenure as a medic. I always wanted my own shop, and now I have one. I took the best of my favorite careers and found a way to combine them. I just may have the only "Bikeulance" on the planet.
