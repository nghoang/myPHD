HSE – Offshore Medic Training Course

HSE – Offshore Medic Training Course
HSE Offshore Medic Refresher Course

The DDRC HSE Offshore Medic course includes a mixture of theory, practical scenarios and student placement in working medical/clinical departments. Two weeks are classroom based whilst the other two weeks are clinical attachments with a General Practitioner and a Hospital Department working with the Nursing and Medical Staff and employing your practical skills. Other opportunities exist in areas of dentistry, confined spaces and rope-access along with risk assessment and health and safety management issues. It will also include refresher training on CPR, first aid, AED, oxygen administration, Immediate Life Support and intravenous/osseous techniques.

Prerequisites/Entry Requirements

Candidates must meet the criteria set out in the Offshore Installations and Pipeline Works (First Aid) Regulations 1989, (SI 1989 No. 1671), that is either:

  • Registered Nurse - Adult, who appear on the NMC register, and have a suitable
    portfolio with at least 3 years post registration experience.
  • RMN’s with other nursing qualifications, (RGN), who appear on the NMC register and have a suitable portfolio with at least 3 years post registration in general nursing.
  • Naval Medical Assistants, who have attained the rate of Leading Medical Assistant and have a suitable portfolio with at least 5 years service.
  • Army Medical Assistants (CMT1, RMA1), who have attained the rank of Corporal and have a suitable portfolio with at least 5 years service.
  • RAF Medical Assistants, who have attained the rank of Corporal and have a suitable portfolio with at least 5 years service.

Course Duration

  • The full course requires 20 working days training (150 contact hours including examination) and is split into four weeks training.

Certification

  • HSE Offshore Medic certificate

Testimonial

I was a student for the HSE Offshore Medic course at DDRC in 2010. I came from Canada for the course as I have been working at remote and offshore sites for the last 3 years and no such course exists at home.
I was thoroughly impressed with the DDRC, the Faculty, Facility and Program was top notch. I had a unique perspective coming into the course as I have already worked as an Offshore Medic in Canadian water, the information was relevant and practical to the offshore environment and the instructors were experienced and intelligent. The clinical placements were above and beyond what I expected to experience. I especially found the clinical placement in the Emergency Dentistry Clinic to be valuable to the offshore role. I would highly recommend DDRC to anyone looking at taking any of the courses they offer.
Jonathon MacKay
