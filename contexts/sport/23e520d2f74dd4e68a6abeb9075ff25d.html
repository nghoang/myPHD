Tuesday November 06 2012

IRELAND'S Olympic silver medalist John Joe Nevin has backtracked on plans to become a professional boxer.

The Mullingar native has instead signed up to fight for the British Lionhearts in the World Series of Boxing next season.

He will be joined on the amateur Lionhearts team by European champion Joe Ward but, most significantly, his decision to remain amateur could see him fight at his third Olympics in 2016.

Nevin had previously announced he would sign a professional contract with former world champion Amir Khan’s newly formed Super Fight Promotions.

However, he did not sign a contract and has now decided to remain amateur.

The Lionhearts will face up against teams from Germany, Italy, Kazakhstan, Ukraine and the USA in Group B with contests consisting of five fights, at five different weights.
