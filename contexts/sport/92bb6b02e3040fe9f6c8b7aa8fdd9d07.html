Shelly-Ann out of Athlete-of-the-Year contest

Tuesday, November 06, 2012 | 1:21 PM


Tweet

KINGSTON, Jamaica - Back to back 100 metre Olympic champion, Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce failed to make the final three of the 2012 female World Athlete of the Year.

Valerie Adams of New Zealand, American Allyson Felix and Britain’s Jessica Ennis were given the nod by the judges.

The award is presented by the International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF).

Adams is the back-to-back Olympic shot put champion and also won this year’s Diamond League crown, capping an unbeaten season.

Felix won three gold medals in London, helping the US to both sprint-relay gold medals and finally winning the Olympic 200m after two second place finishes to Jamaica’s Veronica Campbell-Brown, who finished fourth.

Ennis, the face of Britain’s Olympic Games, is the reigning heptathlon champion who also got a pentathlon silver at the World Indoor Championship earlier this year.

The voting process to determine the three male and three female finalists for the World Athletes of the Year Award started on Monday 8 October and concluded on Sunday 28 October.

Votes were cast from a panel of 2,400 people around the globe representing the IAAF & IAF Council Members, the 212 IAAF Member Federations, the IAAF Committee & Commission Members, the One-day Meeting Directors, the IAAF Ambassadors, the IAAF Registered Athletes’ Representatives, International Top Athletes, selected members of the International Press and IAAF Staff Members.

Each person voted for one man and one woman. The three men and three women who received the most votes were selected as finalists.

Jamaica’s Usain Bolt was announced as one of the male finalists yesterday. The double sprint world-record holder will be up against Kenya’s 800m world record-holder David Rudisha and 110m hurdles record-holder, American Aries Merritt.

The winners will be announced in Barcelona, Spain on November 24.
