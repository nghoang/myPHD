Roger Federer carves Janko Tipsarevic apart to set ATP Tour Finals record with 40th win

As the winner of the last two Barclays ATP World Tour Finals, Roger Federer simply loves this court.

Coasting: Roger Federer lost four games on way to win over Janko Tipsarevic  Photo: AP

By Simon Briggs

3:47PM GMT 06 Nov 2012

Comments

He began his search for a “threepeat” this afternoon, and was quickly into his stride, disposing of Serbian outsider Janko Tipsarevic in just 68 minutes.

When Federer pulled out of last week’s Paris Masters, there must have been some concern among the organisers here that he would not arrive in ideal shape for this tournament.

They needn’t have worried. From the first game, Federer was timing the ball so sweetly that it recalled his 60-minute demolition of Rafael Nadal here last year.

In fact it was Tipsarevic who was struggling, having picked up a bug in Paris that forced him to withdraw from Saturday’s semi-final against Jerzy Janowicz with only a few points left to play.

He didn’t look entirely fit, but even if he had been, it would have been virtually impossible to deal with Federer in this mood.

This low-bouncing court suits Federer’s game, because it saves him from having to play the awkward high backhand that Nadal has exploited in the past.

And we already know how much he likes playing indoors, after watching what happened when the Centre Court roof closed during this year’s Wimbledon final.

Without any wind or sun to disrupt his serving action, Federer hits the corner of the service box with uncanny regularity.

The best stat from today’s match was that Federer landed 29 first serves and won 28 of those points. He did not face a single break point on the way to his 6-3, 6-1 victory.

The race for No. 1 is already finished for the year, because Federer cannot overhaul Novak Djokovic when he is defending maximum points from last year’s trip to the O2.

From Djokovic’s perspective, that is just as well.
