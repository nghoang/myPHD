SUV: 2013 Lexus RX F Sport

2013 Lexus RX 350 F Sport

Photograph by: Handout , Lexus

Three years into its latest redesign, the RX is getting new makeup and, in at least one case, the makeover is actually adding a soupcon of sexiness to the 2013 model - the F Sport version of the RX 350.

Then you'll get all manner of GS 350-inspired aggressive additions, including the angular trapezoidal grille. There's also some big 19-inch wheels with P235/55R/19 tires.

Inside, the interior's subdued luxury is further highlighted by a few modest aluminum bits and some tastefully subtle wood trim. What really sets the new RX apart, however, is its centre console. Other than the air conditioner controls and some minimalistic radio switchgear, the centre console is mercifully devoid of extra buttons, the cleanest, by far, of any recent luxury vehicle I can remember.

The powertrain remains typical Lexus - Toyota's ubiquitous 3.5-litre V6, in this guise claiming a 270-horsepower peak output. Thanks to a generous amount of torque - 248 pound-feet - and a slick-shifting eight-speed transmission that keeps the engine in the meat of its powerband, the F Sport doesn't often have to be spun to its 6,400-rpm redline.

Lexus's handling upgrades include a trick new flexible suspension mounting system that Lexus claims allows the F Sport to use a firmer suspension without cramping up the ride. For the most part, it works - less body roll without noticeable degradation in ride quality. Throw in weightier steering and the F Sport, if not nearly Porsche Cayenne material, is still sportier than much of its competition.

The all-wheel-drive system, as in previous Lexuses, defaults to front-wheel-drive power transfer, though it can send 50% of the engine's torque rearward if wheel slippage is detected by the traction control system.

Lexus is trying to accomplish what some in marketing say is impossible - appealing to everyone at least part of the time. Rather than focus on just being sporty (a la BMW), versatile (Audi) or conventional (Lexus for the last decade or so), it is striving to capture all three. It will be a tough job to capture these markets and everything in between.

But as the versatility of the RX platform - the basic 350, the frugal RX 450h hybrid and now this decidedly more aggressive F Sport - shows, it is expanding its product portfolio to seek a wider audience.
