A Difference A Year Doesn’t Make

;

;

So despite all the changes the Mets have made, the success of their starting pitchers, the growth of their young and up coming hitters, the Mets sit here, on July 19th, with the same exact record as last season. From this point last season the Mets went 31-43 and we all hope that is not where things are headed again this year, although in the midst of a season long 6 game losing streak, things are looking quite bleak.

With the 35th pick in the 2012 First-Year Player Draft the Mets select…

Kevin Plawecki, catcher from Purdue, with the pick received as compensation from the Miami Marlins for signing Jose Reyes.  He is 6’1″, 215 lbs, and bats and throws right handed.  In his junior season at Purdue, he struck out just 8 times in 211 at-bats.

According to PurdueSports.com;

“2012 Season: Big Ten Player of the Year… Big Ten Tournament Most Outstanding Player… National Semifinalist for the Golden Spikes Award, Dick Howser Trophy and Johnny Bench Award…Collegiate Baseball Louisville Slugger Second Team All-America…First Team All-Big Ten… NCAA All-Gary Regional Team… only the fifth player since 1981 to be the Big Ten Player/Pitcher of the Year and Tournament MOP in the same season… third player in program history to be a two-time First Team All-Big Ten honoree.. most difficult batter to strikeout in the Big Ten again… homered twice at the Big Ten Tournament, giving Purdue the lead in the late innings with both long balls… one of only two players nationally that is a semifinalist for all three prominent awards… complete season recap to follow.”
