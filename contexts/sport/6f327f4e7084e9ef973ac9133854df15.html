Gordon youngsters are in2cricket

  • Cricket

7 Nov 12 @ 03:21am by Torin Chen

  • Gordon in2cricket juniors. Picture: ELENOR TEDENBORG

  • Gordon in2cricket juniors. Picture: ELENOR TEDENBORG

  • Gordon in2cricket juniors. Picture: ELENOR TEDENBORG

more photos

DOZENS of boys and girls are flocking to the Gordon junior cricket club to take part in the popular in2cricket program.

The program provides a simple and fun introduction to the game for children aged five to eight.

HAVE YOUR SAY: What do you think of the in2cricket program? COMMENT BELOW

The children learn how to bat, bowl and field with coloured balls, bats and cricket stumps.

Gordon in2cricket co-ordinator Mark Ham said the program had helped the junior club grow from zero to 100 participants in just three years.

"In2cricket is a fun-based program for five- to eight-year-olds," he said.

"Everyone learns new skills each week in a happy and safe environment.

"We have players of all standards, shapes and sizes, we cater for everyone.

"All we care about is fostering everyone having a go and giving cricket a try."

Gordon's season commenced at Beauchamp Oval, Chatswood, last month and the club would welcome more players.

Senior Gordon club players Kirsten Levy and Felicity Trinh have coached some of the children.

"We have a great group of parent helpers and coaches," Ham said.

"All parents are very much encouraged to join in and we provide them with coaching tips and assistance throughout.

"Who knows? We could be seeing the next Michael Clarke or Lisa Sthalekar play in2cricket this season?"

For more details, go to gordonjuniorcricket.com
