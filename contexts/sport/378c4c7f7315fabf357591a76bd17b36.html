MANCHESTER CITY 2 AJAX 2

21.39 Another 2-2 draw in tonight's other game in Group D, which leaves the group pretty much as it was to begin with tonight. City will need two wins from Real Madrid at home and Dortmund away. Not a likely outcome if they continue to defend basic situations so poorly. They were good for spells tonight, re-energised after half-time and encouraged by the ease with which exciting things started happening when they switched to a more direct game in the latter stages. But there's still a sense of them worryingly below par this season. We're yet to see that thrilling side of a year ago, nor the ultra-determined one that won the league in the final months of the 2011/12 season.

That'll be all from this liveblog, thanks for your company, I'll talk to you again soon.

21.34 Major controversy at the end, with City denied what looks like a penalty in the final act of the game! Balotelli was clearly having his shirt pulled as he tried to bring down a high ball, but Peter Rasmussen blew the final whistle instead of pointing to the spot. Some bottling may have occured there.

FULL TIME

90+2 min Ajax cocner. Finally they put one straight into the box. Fischer's on the end of it, but his shot goes out for a throw.

90 min Three added minutes. Fischer on the attack for Ajax from the left but finds only the side netting.

88 min City very unfortunate not to be in front. Dzeko holds it up on the left before playing in Kolarov who squares it for Aguero. He finishes high past Vermeer but it's ruled out for offisde, probably against Kolarov, but it's very tight indeed and, I'm gonna say, wrong.

87 min Kolarov picks out Kompany with a header from the left. His header takes it away from goal. City maintain possession. Kolarov tries a low cross from the left. Dzeko can only deflect it behind. Poulsen coming off for Ajax, Fischer on.

85 min Poulsen booked for a cynical foul on Balotelli who had got past him 30 yards from goal. Kolarov is on for Gareth Barry.

81 min City nearly caught out as Ajax break. Eriksen with a neat turn which almost deceives Kompany, but not enough for the prodigous Dane to find Poulsen after a run from deep which left him unmarked in the middle. Here's Henry Winter:

79 min Nasri pings a free kick in from the right towards the far post. Balotelli rises highest and heads it towards the opposite corner from the left-hand side of the area. Great diving save from Vermeer!

77 min We have a clear solution to City's ills, and it's hitting the ball very hard and very fast up the field. Suddenly it's all chances, and strikers on the ball in the box. Aguero is judged offside after Clichy heads it into the box.

74 min GOAL!! Man City 2 Ajax 2 (Aguero)
It's that man again! Just as the game and City's Champions League hopes looked to be drifting towards doom, they go route one and get instant results. Huge hoof upfield, Balotelli flicks on and it drops invitingly for Aguero who is away off Moisander's shoulder. No question that he's going to find the bottom corner given the time he's allowed, and his position inside the box, and he manages not to slip over and equalise.

72 min Another corner, which Ajax again take short. It's like they've made a deal to go easy on Mancini's team this half.

71 min Superb shot from De Jong! Free kick wide on the right, must be 35 yards from goal, and he absolutely hammers it round the wall and brings a marvellous diving save from Hart, who tips it round the post.

70 min City break, to little effect. Momentum draining from their game again. What is up with them?

69 min Ooof, Zabaleta nearly steers past his own keeper after good work down the right from the impressive Van Rhijn. Gets it right, though, it's behind for a corner.

66 min Dzeko on for Tevez for City. Paul Hayward on the many slips of Sergio Aguero:

64 min Ajax surprisingly opt to take a short corner, when City are roughly afraid as crosses as they are the Financial Fair Play rules. Clichy's out to close it down and the chance has gone.

62 min Zabaleta and Barry with more good-not-great from City. Vermeer, a man dead keen to get off his line whenever possible, is out to deal with the attempted through ball. Here's what amounts to a consolation from Yaya Toure as things stand:

60 min Aguero slips over again in a promising position! He rounds Vermeer and tries to turn as he heads towards the byline, but loses his footing at the crucial moment. More annoyance for City.

59 min It's been a far better start to this half from City, but I'm still feeling mildly peeved with their lack of fluency in attack. There are plenty of blind alleys being run down, and a sense of impatience and desperation to their play.

55 min Toure wins a corner after breezing past Poulsen and almost reaching the byline. His cross is sent behind, and Tevez get three chances to cross, including the original corner, as the ball is repeatedly cleared back to him. He can't find a useful ball into the box, but City keep the ball.

54 min Toure takes it and he hits the wall. Eventually breaks for Clichy who hooks it over the defence for Aguero. He finishes past Vermeer but is offside.

53 min City free kick 25 yards out. Balotelli wants it. Of course he does.

51 min Barry pulls the free kick back to Nastasic who tries a volley from just behind the penalty spot. Well over. Rarely works, that, does it? But it did in Ukraine last weekend:

50 min Aguero, who previously slipped over in a promising position for City, is fouled on the left-hand flank. Alderwiereld is booked. Tevez the next to be fouled, by Eriksen, just outside the box on the left-hand side.

48 min Gary Barry thumps in a free kick from the right-hand side. Too easy for Vermeer, who comes a long way off his line to claim it, and holds his nerve to catch cleanly.

46 min A sub at half-time. Javi Garcia is off after an anonymous 45 minutes. Mario Balotelli is on. Dortmund begin their second half against Real Madrid 2-1 ahead.

20.32 Oddly muted feel to this game now, with Ajax suddenly looking more resolute, and City showing what's become a familiar lack of confidence and composure when faced with a team making it tough for them. Roberto Mancini does not look best pleased, but his scarf is still very nice indeed. See you in 15.

HALF TIME

45 min City corner, swung in promisingly, but there's Vermeer to claw it behind, without much confidence.

42 min City back to where they started before the first goal of the evening. Passing it around without much hope or purpose, mostly in their own half. Ajax defending well, and making it difficult for City to get the ball back after losing it.

39 min Zabaleta picks out Toure with a high ball from the right. He heads straight at Vermeer. It's gone a little flat, in a time you'd be expecting tremendous noise levels. Mark Ogden picks up the thread:

35 min It's 1-1 between Real Madrid and Borussia Dortmund in tonight's other game in this group. Just FYI your information.

32 min Zabaleta the most potent attacking threat again for City, on the end of a ball into the box then forcing a firm save from Vermeer. Some indication that City are going to pull it back before half time. Plenty that ITV's panel are going to rituaistically sacrifice the concept of zonal marking to the footballing gods.

28 min Good header on the line from Van Rhijn to deny City after determined running brought Zabaleta into the box. He stood up a ball for Barry on the far post, but Barry fouled Van Rhijn as he guided the ball over the bar for a theoretical corner. Instead it's a free kick to Ajax in their own box.

25 min Ajax enjoying a good spell following the goal. Boerrigter beats Clichy to swing in a cross from the left, but Kompany strides out to clear it from Eriksen. Here are Joe Hart and Pablo Zabaleta looking furious and resigned after conceding the first goal:

22 min GOAL!! Man City 1 Ajax 2 (Toure)
Wow, what a finsih from Toure! He holds off Van Rhijn with ease as he chests down a Tevez knock-down not far from the six yard box. More strength as he turns, keeping his marker at bay, then smashes a volley past Vermeer and into the net. That, as they say in various English-speaking nations around the world, is more like it.

21 min What's extra-annoying for City is that they've not played badly. They've look comfortably the better side, and Ajax have done very little from open play other than win two corners. It's not an easy task for the home side from here.

17 min GOAL!! Man City 0 Ajax 2 (De Jong)
Oh deary, deary me. Eriksen sends in a corner that is no better than reasonable and the marking from City looks like a protest against Roberto Mancini. No-one taking responsibility, no-one attacking the ball, and it's far, far to easy for De Jong to head in his second goal. Well placed to be fair to him, right in the top corner at the near post, but he was more or less unopposed in doing so. Dreadful.

17 min Another Ajax corner.

10 min GOAL!! Man City 0 Ajax 1 (De Jong)
Oh dear City, what on earth has gone on there? Moisander is allowed to get a toe onto the corner from the right, then one of the Ajax supporters in the crowd applies that Harry Potter curse that makes people into statues to the City players. No-one picks up De Jong, and he's able to dink it past Hart, the only City player alert to the danger, and give the Dutch champions the lead.

8 min Zabaleta slips in behind as Tevez pokes a wise ball through traffic. His shot is poor, and over, but there's already been more than enough here to suggest City will be amongst the goals tonight.

7 min Easy enough for Vermeer in the Ajax goal. Ajax, by the way are wearing a neon n navy Tron-indebted kit tonight which is terrible in theory but oddly pleasing in reality.

6 min Nice flowing move forward for City, with Nasri and Aguero in space on the left. Still a bit of slackness with speed of thought and action, as Tevez spreads it towards their side a little too slowly, and Aguero opts to check inside, where his shot is inevitably deflected behind. Corner.

5 min More stability to the game now, City doing their "yep, we're passing it along our defenders, we're a team of experienced European campaigners" thang.

18.45 Evening liveblog fans.

So this is it for Manchester City. The end of the line. The last chance. Do or die. Win or bust. Must-win. No more chances. The definitive moment. The ultimate window. The conclusive egg.

Their Champions League status rests on beating Ajax tonight, after another disappointing showing in the group stage in which they've done a credible impression of English teams playing in European competition after the post-Heysel ban.

City sit dead last in Group D after a lucky draw with Dortmund, a deserved defeat to Ajax and an irritating one to Real Madrid.

A win tonight gives them semi-realistic hopes of another miraculous late turnaround, but on recent evidence they're out of form, short on ideas, and shakier than expected in defence.

Ajax were allowed back into the game rather than there by their own endeavours two weeks ago, and an improved defensive showing should be enough to keep them at bay.

But what of those irritating misfiring attacking players? City could only offer sideways passes and shrugs when West Ham set up to defend against them on Sunday. Ajax, if they fancy doing the same, are likely to be even tougher to break down.
