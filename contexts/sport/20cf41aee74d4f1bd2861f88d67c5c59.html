Jockey praises Cup winner Green Moon

Updated: 07:10, Wednesday November 7, 2012

Loyalty is an important word for Melbourne Cup winning jockey Brett Prebble and that is why Tuesday's success in Australia's biggest race meant so much to the experienced hoop.

The former Melbourne jockey, who now plies his trade in Hong Kong, picked up his first Cup victory with a brilliant ride aboard Green Moon to give owner Lloyd Williams his fourth triumph in the great race.

Prebble has always had a great relationship with the trainers and owners he rides for and it is no different with Williams as the duo combined with great success in the $6.2 million race.

Prebble revealed after the race that he could have easily had the ride on 2011 Cup winner Dunaden or runner-up Red Cadeaux, but his loyalty to Williams cost him the winning mount.

But 12 months later it was a different story as Williams booked Prebble for Green Moon and the rest is history.

'I've had plenty of opportunities but finally it was my turn,' Prebble said.

'Every year I leave it open to him (Williams) and I wait until pretty much the death and last year it cost me first or second because I could've ridden those two (Dunaden and Red Cadeaux).

'So sometime luck is your fortune and I always repay people that are loyal to me.

'To come back this year after the disappointment of last year it's a great feeling.'

Prebble was always confident of winning with Green Moon during the run, despite the fact his horse copped a check early in the race.

'I was very happy with my position after 400m but we got a bit of pressure and they checked him,' he said.

'I just wanted to concentrate on getting him down to the fence so he could switch off and relax.

'I knew he had an awesome turn of foot because I've watched him several times in replays and I knew what I had underneath me and I just needed to conserve his energy.'

Prebble said Green Moon had enough talent to go overseas and win other big races around the world, but that decision would - as always - rest with Williams and trainer Robert Hickmott.

Williams wasn't on track to celebrate alongside Prebble, with the wealthy businessman preferring the low-key atmosphere on his farm in the Victorian town of Macedon to the hustle and bustle at Flemington.

Williams' son Nick represented the family on track and you couldn't hide the smile from his face after the race.

'We put a lot of money in and a heck of a lot of effort and to get this result today is terrific,' Williams junior said.

'This is what we bought him for and this has been the plan.

'I'm just so proud for all our team at the farm (Macedon Lodge) because everyone just puts in so much time and effort, of course led by dad (Lloyd) and it's another Melbourne Cup triumph for Team Williams.'
