IT'S ALL OVER :(

And nothing sums up the mood of Great Britain right now than an unhappy emoticon. I would say something moving about the end of the games but I tried that a few posts back and then the concert kept going for another 40 minutes. I'm worried that if I try it again Brian May might start back up.

Seriously, though, thanks so much for all the emails and comments. Over 3,000 of them on here so sorry I couldn't squeeze many of them in – not least because it kept crashing my computer whenever I tried to load them all!

Thanks for following this blog and for all our Olympic coverage of the past two weeks. The sports desk and beyond did an incredible job covering it all, unlike me who just nipped in at the end to steal all their glory.

Right, I'll leave you all to get weepy with yet another sporting montage. See you down the National Dressage Championships in September, yeah?

Who wants to see the Olympics in numbers? Brought to you by the number crunchers at Press Association ...

  • 3 - Britain's position in the medals table
  • 4 - skeletons found in the ancient settlement on the site of the Olympic Park and carefully removed.
  • 4 - the number of records broken by Team GB.
  • 6 - the number of gold medals won by Sir Chris Hoy, making him the most successful British Olympian ever.
  • 15 - the number of sports Britain has won medals in.
  • 22 - the number of medals won by Michael Phelps, including 18 gold, making him the most decorated Olympian ever.
  • 65 - the number of medals won by Team GB at London 2012.
  • 1,000 - picnic benches at the park.
  • 10,000 the number of temporary toilets at the Olympic Park.
  • 45,000 - the rough number of meals per day for athletes - consisting of 100 tonnes of meat, 25,000 loaves, 21 tonnes of cheese, 82 tonnes of seafood, 300 tonnes of fruit and vegetables.
  • 70,000 - number of volunteer Games Makers.
  • 80,000 - capacity of the Olympic Stadium.
  • 80,000 - the number of spectators that watched the women's football final at Wembley, a record breaking crowd for a women's fixture.
  • 300,000 - nails used to build the Velodrome.
  • 2,400,000 - the number of people who visited the Olympic Park in Stratford.
  • 4,500,000 - the number of people who travelled on the Tube on August 7, the most passengers in one day in London Underground's history.
  • 7,400,000 - the number of people visiting venues across the UK.
  • 10,000,000 - litres of water used in the Aquatics Centre.
  • 13,000,000 - the number of people who saw the Olympic Flame during the Torch Relay.
  • 22,400,000 - the average UK TV audience.
  • 1,000,000,000 - Up to 1 billion people worldwide watched the opening ceremony alone.

Hannah Waldram has been venturing into various comment sections across the Guardian to find out what you're all thinking about the show tonight, as well as the games in general ...

SDU1969 says: "The Spice Girls should be fun and they are far more well known around the world than a lot of our other performers. Adele would have been good too. A duet would be unlikely though? As long as we don't get too much of Seb Coe. Ugh. I want to slap him everytime he opens his mouth."

I'm not sure that slapping Seb Coe is really in the spirit of the games now, is it? Although if it was an Olympic event I know a few people who would be striving for that gold medal.

Elsewhere lovejoype9 is at the Blur concert in Hyde Park to celebrate the closing ceremony there. "Went to the Olympic Park on the first Saturday and watched handball and basketball. It feels like an age ago. So many wonderful memories. Have been glued Guardian blog and the live coverage. What a feast. I really don't want it to end. Like all good parties they have to but is has been fabulous, uplifting, life affirming and utterly brilliant."

And finally flusturedmum is a little concerned about the closing ceremony. "Hopefully, if it is a bit dodgy we'll all see it through the Olympic afterglow. But if it's anything even close to the opening one, there should be nothing to worry about."

6.46pm BST

Updated at 6.47pm BST

So what can we expect from the closing ceremony? To be fair, Arnold has made it clear that they won't be a attempting to recreate the reflective masterpiece that was Boyle's opening bang. Rather, this will be a "cheeky and cheesy" celebration of the best of British music and humour which, let's be honest, could go either way.

We're promised "a symphony of British music”, with everything from Elgar to Liam Gallagher in the process. 4,100 performers (including 3,500 volunteers) have, apparently, been rehearsing for the show inside Dagenham’s Ford car plant and it's rumoured that we'll see performances from the Spice Girls (reforming and singing on top of black cabs), Kate Bush (who's barely done a show since 1979) and George Michael, singing for the first time since a life-threatening illness late last year.

“If the opening ceremony was the wedding, then we’re the wedding reception,” is how Arnold sums the whole thing up. Which presumably means we’ll get a load of people dancing badly to Dancing Queen and Come On Eileen while someone’s uncle acts like a bit of a dick in the photos.

6.33pm BST

I'd like to think I speak on behalf of the entire nation when I say this: What on EARTH are we all going to do next week? Yes, tonight's closing ceremony signifies what we've all been dreading for at least 13 days now … it's the end of the Olympics. We're all going to have to go back to reading about wars and hating on politicians. There will be no women's judo to distract us, and not a single horse tap dancing for our amusement … I'm not sure how we will cope …

… But! It's not all over just yet. I'll be here like the hype man I so nearly was, warming you all up for the closing ceremony with some preview and gossip. I'll also be chatting on about the various Olympic moments that have made me – and you – weep like pathetic babies over the last couple of weeks so do leave some of your highlights in the comments below or tweet me at @timjonze

From 9pm I will also be guiding you through yet another big bash at the Olympic Stadium which will (hopefully) be as mesmerising as Danny Boyle's opener. Basically, all David Arnold (musical director, serial Bond scorer) and Kim Gavin (creative director, puts on big flashy Take That concerts) have to do to top Boyle's effort is bring us together as a nation, annoy a selection of Tory politicians with BLATANT LEFTIST PROPAGANDA, hurl the Queen out of a helicopter AND reform the Spice Girls. No pressure, like …

6.04pm BST
