Sport

Sport

The children in years 3 to 6 take part in a broad range of sports including netball, soccer, rugby, hockey, cricket, basketball, cross country running and swimming.  We invite experts into the school  to give both children and staff ideas and practical experience to help develop particular sports.  In recent years courses run by  these specialists include, soccer and rugby.  Staff have also attended courses on health and related fitness, soccer, netball, basketball and general outdoor activities during the past few years.

We have school teams for soccer, netball, cross country and athletics.  These teams compete against local schools regularly.  In addition children take part in award schemes for cycle training.

During our yearly residential visits and day visits we have also given the children sporting experiences such as climbing, abseiling, canoeing, raft building, problem solving, night walking, mountain walking, caving and team building.

We also encourage children to join local soccer, rugby, athletics and cricket clubs.

Our sports facilities include a hard surface area for games such as netball, hockey and basketball, a large field which is marked out for soccer or track athletics and an indoor hall in which there is a climbing frame, a set of ropes and other apparatus.  All KS2 children are taken swimming at Daventry Leisure Centre for weekly lessons for approximately half of the school year.

Children are encouraged to use small P.E. equipment, in a sensible manner, during playtimes.
