O’Sullivan takes year out and misses defence

Hector Nunns
Published 1 minute ago

Ronnie O’Sullivan yesterday withdrew from the remainder of the calendar for the season, meaning he will be taking a full year away from the sport and therefore not even defending the World Championship title he won in such spectacular fashion in May. Although Sullivan, 37 next month and down to twentieth in the world rankings, has stopped short of retiring altogether, the decision clearly raises the question of whether he will ever again appear at a leading tournament or even be seen playing professional snooker. O’Sullivan’s intention, formally announced in a brief statement by World Snooker, the governing body, will come as a shock to many, although it is not a total surprise, given the six-month sabbatical he was already on, his late withdrawal from last week’s “comeback” event at the International Championship in China and a well-documented long battle with depression. It is believed that a recurrence of that condition is a factor in his decision. His hand on the timing was forced, with the authorities seeking assurances about his playing intentions before next month’s UK Championship in York and January’s Masters at Alexandra Palace. The first-round draws for both will be released later this week. It is ironic that O’Sullivan’s biggest struggle comes after one of his greatest triumphs.
