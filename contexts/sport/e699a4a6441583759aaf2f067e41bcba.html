Arsenal pegged back by Schalke as the pair vie for Group A top spot

Schalke scored either side of half-time to cancel out Arsenal's two early goals as both sides look to end the night in pole position in Group A.

Theo Walcott makes his first Champions League start of the season against Schalke tonight (Picture: AP)

A quick-fire double from Arsenal set Arsene Wenger's side well on the way to finishing the night as Group A leaders, but Schalke hit back right on the stroke of half-time to keep them in the hunt before equalising midway through the second half.

Walcott was the first to strike as he netted his eighth goal in 12 appearances this season, before Olivier Giroud produced a superb diving header to reward Lukas Podolski's persistence on the left wing.

But what looked as though it was going to be a comfortable score-line at the break for the visitors suddenly became less certain as Schalke's Klaas-Jan Huntelaar fired past Vito Mannone after a superb volleyed pass from Lewis Holtby.

And Jefferson Farfan's leveller ensured a nervy last 20 minutes for the travelling Arsenal contingent.

Gunners' defender Andre Santos was left out of the starting line-up after angering boss Arsene Wenger at the weekend.

The Brazilian infuriated his manager on Saturday when he swapped shirts with former Gunner Robin van Persie at half-time during their 2-1 defeat to Manchester United, leaving him to issue an apology for his unprofessional actions.

But his grovelling fell on deaf ears as Wenger left him on the bench, while in form Theo Walcott was given the nod for his first start in Europe this season.

The change meant captain Thomas Vermaelen switched to left back, with Laurent Koscielny filling in at centre-half.

Arsenal have lost three of their last five matches, including the reverse fixture against Schalke two weeks ago, but if they can hang on for victory in Gelsenkirchen they will go above the Bundesliga side at the top of Group A and have qualification for the knock-out stages all but assured.
