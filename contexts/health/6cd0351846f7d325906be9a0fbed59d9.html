Free Dental Care

Toronto Public Health has a new fully-equipped mobile dental clinic that travels to select community agencies across the city. It provides free dental care for eligible clients who have difficulty accessing dental services in the customary manner. For more information and to access this service, please call 311.

Flu Shot Clinic Schedule

Get a free flu shot at one of our clinics across Toronto. The flu shot is also available at your doctor's office and other health care institutions. Get it early to protect yourself and others.

November is Diabetes Awareness Month

Diabetes is a serious disease, but it can be prevented. Small changes towards a healthy lifestyle can prevent or delay the start of type 2 diabetes if you are at risk for the disease.

Health Canada Lifts Suspension of Flu Vaccine

On October 31, 2012, Health Canada lifted the suspension of the distribution and use of Novartis manufactured flu vaccine (Agriflu and Fluad) after a thorough investigation. None of the information reviewed indicated a safety issue. Flu vaccines continue to be safe and effective; they are your best defence against the flu.

Number and Size Matter: Know Your Alcohol Limits

Drinking beyond the recommended alcohol limits can put you at risk for short and long term harm. Learn about Canada's Low-Risk Alcohol Drinking Guidelines.

HPV Vaccine Program Expansion for Females born 1993-1999

Free HPV vaccine is available for females up to Grade 12 who have not completed the Grade 8 HPV vaccine series. Details and clinic schedule are available.

Mental Health Resources and Services

In the month of November, the Movember campaign brings awareness to men's health issue, specifically prostate cancer and male mental health initiatives. Check out Toronto Public Health's mental health resources and services.

Food Recall on Beef Products

The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) is warning the public, distributors and food service establishments not to consume, sell, or serve the beef products described in the CFIA food recall because the products may be contaminated with E. coli O157:H7.

Public Health Information

Call 3-1-1 for information and referral to Toronto Public Health programs and services.

Visit the Canadian Food Inspection Agency website for information on food recalls.
