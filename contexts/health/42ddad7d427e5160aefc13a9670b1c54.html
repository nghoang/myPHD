New rural health committee established

07 Nov, 2012 04:00 AM
THE State government has established a new Ministerial Advisory Committee for Rural Health to improve health and health care across rural NSW.

Health Minister Jillian Skinner, who made the announcement in Wagga Wagga yesterday, said the committee would be made up of rural clinicians, community members and members of local health districts, and would advise the minister on how to improve health outcomes in rural NSW.

ÒThe Ministerial Advisory Committee will work closely with the Parliamentary Secretary for Regional Health, Melinda Pavey, to identify methods to improve patient services in rural NSW," Ms Skinner said.

To support the Ministerial Advisory Committee, a Rural Health Unit will be established within the NSW Ministry of Health to ensure recommendations of the committee encourage the development of rural health policies, are monitored and evaluated.

ÒThe Rural Health Unit will support the Ministerial Advisory Committee and develop appropriate rural health policy initiatives,Ó Mrs Skinner said.

ÒIn addition to the Rural Health Unit, the Agency for Clinical Innovation will develop a Rural Health Network, which will play a vital role in co-ordinating models of care in rural areas and advising how they can best be implemented by local health districts."
