Grand Challenges in Global Health

The Grand Challenges in Global Health initiative is focused on engaging creative minds to work on scientific and technological breakthroughs for the world’s most pressing health problems. Grand Challenges Explorations is a grant program within the initiative that fosters innovative, early-stage research to expand the pipeline of ideas that can lead to those much needed global health and development solutions.
Apply Now for Grand Challenges Explorations and the TB Vaccine Accelerator Program.
 Learn more

Grand Challenges Explorations Funds Over 90 New Projects

A diverse group of innovators from around the world are poised to test their bold ideas to improve global health and development through Grand Challenges Explorations. Over 90 grantees have been awarded $100,000 each to explore early-stage projects focusing on protecting plant crops, optimizing immunization systems, exploring new global health solutions, developing new methods to screen anti-malarial compounds, and identifying new ways to communicate that foreign aid is working. In addition, 15 Grand Challenges Explorations researchers have received Phase II funding of up to $1 million to continue their projects.

Featured below are some examples of the new projects awarded Grand Challenges Explorations grants. For a complete list of the over 800 projects awarded to date, visit the Grand Challenges Explorations web site. Two-page applications for Grand Challenges Explorations Round 10 are being accepted until November 7, 2012 at 11:30 a.m. Pacific Time.

  A Brand to Communicate About Aid
Jack McPartland of Future Buro in Australia will work to turn the figure “0.7%” – the United Nations target for aid donations from the Gross Domestic Product of developed countries – into a brand for stories telling how this small fraction can make a big impact.
Vaccine Reminder Ankle Bands
Noor Sabah Rakhshani of the Trust for Vaccines and Immunization in Pakistan will develop ankle bands for children with indicators that change color at specified time intervals to remind mothers of their children’s vaccine due dates.
Testing Anti-malarial Compounds in MosquitoesKoen Dechering of TropIQ Health Sciences in the Netherlands will use malaria parasites that emit light as they develop in their life cycle after ingestion by mosquitoes to allow easy identification of compounds that block malaria transmission.
Molecular Camouflage for Crop Plants
James Rogers of aPEEL Technology in the United States will use natural plant extracts that could coat crops and prevent their recognition by bacterial, fungal, and insect pests.
