Earth Fare health-foods supermarket opening Wednesday in Noblesville

Nov 6, 2012   |

Written by
T.J. Banes

  • Filed Under
  • Communities
  • Hamilton County

A new health-focused supermarket -- Earth Fare -- opens its first Indiana location on Wednesday at Hamilton Town Center.

The 26,000-square-foot facility is the 29th location in the country, featuring more than 1,000 private-label offerings -- 70 percent of which are organic.

Fresh produce, natural meats, and an on-site bakery and caf? will open to the public at 8 a.m. daily. Beginning Thursday and every Thursday, families can feed up to six children (12 and younger) for free with the purchase of a $5 meal. Regular entrees include homemade pizza, soups, sandwiches and whole rotisserie chickens.

The store also feeds about 4,000 kids a week for free across the country.

And just in time for Thanksgiving, the store offers plenty of natural turkeys. Shoppers can also take part in the "Give. Thanks" campaign providing free turkeys to deserving families. Last year, the store donated 2,200 throughout the country. This year, the goal is 3,000.

Earth Fare store hours are 8 a.m. to 9 p.m., Monday through Saturday and 9 a.m. to 9 p.m., Sunday. For more information, visit www.earthfare.com.
