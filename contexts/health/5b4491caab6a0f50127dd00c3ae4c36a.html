Efforts to improve health service of pastoral community bearing fruit

Jijjiga, November 6 (WIC) ÐThe efforts made by the government to benefit the pastoral community from health care service have born encouraging results, according the Somali State Health Bureau.

Bureau Deputy Head Dr Yuosuf Mohammed told WIC today following the efforts made by the government, health service coverage in the state has reached to 74 per cent.

According to Dr Yuosuf, some 1,060 health posts, 121 health centers and 8 hospitals are currently providing heath care services for the pastoral community at their full capacity.

As a result of the expansion of health care coverage, death rate has declined in the state compared to the previous years, he indicated.

Moreover, the health extension program underway since 1999 EC has registered remarkable results in reducing child and maternal mortality in the state, he added. About 1,960 health workers are currently providing health care services.

Similarly, the Jijiga Meles Zenawi referral hospital being built with over 181 million birr is expected to goes operational by the end of this budget year, Dr Yuosuf noted.
