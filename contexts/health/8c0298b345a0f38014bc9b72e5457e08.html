Over the counter drugs raise alarms

7.30 Report - 06/11/2012
Emergency rooms and clinics report an increase in people needing help for the misuse of over the counter painkillers, particularly codeine and ibuprofen, leading to calls for a review of how they're sold.

Saliva reduces pain of DNA collection

Science Online - 06/11/2012
Saliva could provide a less painful and more convenient way of collecting DNA for research, scientists say.

Claims Queensland's disability system is in crisis

AM - 06/11/2012
People with disabilities and support groups in Queensland say the state's disability system is in crisis, and it doesn't have the funding to provide for many people's basic needs. In one case a man with a degenerative disease has had to apply to charities to repair his wheelchair because of a lack of government funding. The Queensland Government is the only state insisting that it can't afford to take part in trials for the proposed national disability insurance scheme.

Pike River disaster report calls for mine safety reform

AM - 06/11/2012
The final report on the 2010 Pike River mine disaster warns New Zealanders they cannot afford to ignore the lessons of past tragedies. With government, unions, and the families of disaster victims all calling for change, the Pike River Royal Commission may usher in sweeping health and safety reforms.

Union warns asbestos in substations could be nationwide problem

AM - 06/11/2012
Ausgrid claims that most of the 35 electrical substations in Sydney's Central Business District with friable asbestos have been cleaned - at night by workers in full protective suits to avoid alarming members of the public. The Electrical Trades Union's NSW Secretary Steve Butler warns the problem will not be just in Sydney, but nationwide, with more than 50 Ausgrid employees already developing asbestos disease, and the toll growing by at least two a year.

Brain fragments when you 'go under'

Science Online - 06/11/2012
The unconscious brain is fragmented into tiny regions that are 'awake' but unable to talk to each other, according to a study looking at the effects of general anaesthetic.

Does Australia expose children to unhealthy lead levels?

7.30 Report - 05/11/2012
A group of environmental scientists say Australia lags behind other developed countries in acceptable levels of lead exposure, arguing that up to 100,000 children here may have elevated levels of the heavy metal in their blood.

Futile interventions question medical system's focus

7.30 Report - 05/11/2012
Some families are asking whether our health system encourages medical intervention over palliative care.

Patients suffering futile treatment

PM - 05/11/2012
Some patients are having an unnecessarily invasive and uncomfortable experience instead of palliative care. The problem is blamed on an increasingly death-denying culture and litigation-fearing doctors. But futile medical treatment is costly and critics say Australia can't afford it.

Concerns for Australian kids' high lead levels

AM - 05/11/2012
A group of scientists and health specialists has written in today's Medical Journal of Australia warning that as many as 100,000 Australian children may have blood lead levels high enough to cause health problems. The authors say Australia's lead level 'recommendations' haven't changed in almost 20 years, but the science has.
