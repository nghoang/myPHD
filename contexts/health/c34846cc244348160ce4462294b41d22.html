Top heart rhythm specialist leads debate on treatment

A consultant cardiologist at Papworth Hospital is leading the way in discussing developments for people whose hearts have an irregular beat.

Dr Andrew Grace, described by The Lancet as “one of the world’s leading heart rhythm specialists”, is lead author of a new Lancet series on diagnosis, treatment and biology of cardiac arrhythmias.

Arrhythmia is believed to affect more than one million people in the UK, and is one of the top 10 reasons why people go to hospital.

Dr Grace said progress in the last 20 years had been remarkable.

“Today cardiac electrophysiology, which not so long ago was essentially diagnostic, is now often curative,” he said.

“The current landscape in arrhythmia management would be unrecognisable to practitioners 20 years ago.

“Although we have made massive strides in our understanding and treatment of arrhythmias, there are still gaps. For example, drug treatment remains an issue: the drugs are variably effective and do have side effects.”
