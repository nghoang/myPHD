Health dept. kicking off food safety campaign

Offers 'turkey tips,' meat thermometers for holidays
By Deanna Herbert NCHD public information officerjournal-advocate.com
Posted:   11/06/2012 10:34:06 AM MST

The tradition of producing a holiday feast does not come without its challenges; particularly when it comes to ensuring the safety of the food you're serving. Throughout the month of November environmental health staff from the Northeast Colorado Health Department will be out in area grocery stores promoting their annual Turkey Tips Food Safety Education Campaign. Staff will be handing out free food safety information and magnets, as well as a limited number of food thermometers at each location.

In its third year, the program has been possible due in large part to a $2,500 grant the agency receives from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. This year the grant helps NCHD to implement and meet Standard 7 of the FDA Voluntary Program Standards in Retail Food, which is industry and consumer interaction and educational outreach.

"This time of year presents more of a challenge in the kitchen because holiday meals typically include a larger amount of food, and often times people are unfamiliar with how some of those foods should be cooked or how long they can sit out on the table," said Carmen Vandenbark, NCHD's director of environmental health. "This program gives us the opportunity to get out into our communities and visit with the average consumer who may not be as well versed on food safety, proper holding temperatures, and contamination dangers as some of our commercial facilities. Many people already know some of the information, but a little reminder won't hurt, especially if it helps prevent food poisoning. We encourage consumers to come to one of the locations to take advantage of the free thermometers and visit with us about any kind of food safety."

NCHD staff will be at the these locations and dates in November:

Tuesday, Nov. 13: Edwards Right Price Market, Fort Morgan, 10 a.m. to noon; Fort Morgan Safeway, 1:30 to 3:30 p.m.

Wednesday, Nov. 14: Bella's Market, Wray, 8 to 10 a.m.; Shop All, Yuma, 2 to 4 p.m., Fort Morgan Wal-Mart, 1:30 to 3:30 p.m

Thursday, Nov. 15: Sterling Wal-Mart, 8:30 to 10:30 a.m.; Sunmart, 1 to 3 p.m.; Sterling Grocery Mart, 3 to 5 p.m.; Julesburg Cafe, 8:45 to 10:45 a.m.; Holyoke Marketplace, 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m.; Bella's Market, Haxtun, 2:30 to 4:30 p.m.

Friday, November 16: Brush Grocery Kart, 3 to 6 p.m.

Monday, November 19: Bella's Market, Akron, 5 to 6:30 p.m.

For more information on how to make your holiday feast a safe one, visit nchd.org.
