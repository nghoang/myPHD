Health & Behavioural Sciences

The Faculty of Health & Behavioural Sciences includes three Schools, each of which offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. The Faculty also supports a variety of research areas.

Welcome

The Faculty of Health and Behavioural Sciences is comprised of three Schools, School of Health Sciences; School of Nursing, Midwifery and Indigenous Health; and School of Psychology. Each of these schools offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs designed to meet the needs of a diverse student population. The Faculty employs modern technology and best practice teaching methods essential to the development of quality education.

Grant Successes

NHMRC Successes

Congratulations to Associate Professor Chao Deng (CIA) and Professor Xu-Feng Huang (CIB) who were successful in obtaining a large NHMRC  grant for their project titled  "Roles of muscarinic M3 receptors in antipsychoticinduced metabolic side-effects: prevention and treatment of antipsychotic-induced insulin dysregulation".  

Congratulations also to Dr Deborah Apthorp, School of Psychology, who has been awarded an NHMRC Australian Clinical Fellowship to work (with Rodney Croft) on the project "Effects of electromagnetic radiation on visual processing.
