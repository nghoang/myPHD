Programs & Services

Public Health

The province's health regions work closely with Saskatchewan Health to maintain a system for identifying and dealing with public health risks.

Saskatchewan Health is responsible for ensuring there is a combination of legislation, regulations, programs, services, education and awareness about impediments to good health. In this way, we help maintain healthy people in healthy communities.

Health Promotion Strategy

Programs to promote healthy lifestyles and prevent disease and illness.

Monitoring and Surveillance
The health system monitors population health levels and public health risks.
Population Health Assessment

Research and evaluation to measure health performance and opportunities for quality improvement.

Prevention and Protection

Prevention of disease, illness and injury, and protection from public health risks.

Public Health Offices

Contact information for public health offices in Saskatchewan health regions.

Saskatchewan Disease Control Laboratory

Saskatchewan Disease Control Laboratory (SDCL) provides laboratory surveillance to reduce the risk from new and re-emerging infectious diseases.

Saskatchewan Population Health Council (SPHC)

The Saskatchewan Population Health Council provides direction on public health priorities, strategies, operational processes and accountability measures via multi-sectoral collaboration.
