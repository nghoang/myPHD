Public health nursing services in crisis

Tweet
Tuesday, November 06, 2012

PUBLIC HEALTH nursing services in the county are in crisis, a special meeting between the Institute of Community Health Nursing and public representatives in Roscommon was told on Monday.

County Roscommon is the only county in the country without a Director or Acting Director of Public Health Nursing.

At a special meeting with public representatives on Monday the institute outlined in detail the current challenges facing the sector, which included key staffing vacancies, a lack of clinical support, reduced weekend cover, and the absence of a public health nurse in four out of 20 areas in the county.

The meeting was attended by Deputies Frank Feighan and Denis Naughten, Senators Terry Leyden and John Kelly, and Council-lors Laurence Fallon and Paddy Kilduff.

There are currently nine public health nursing vacancies in the county due to retirements, long sick leave, and maternity leave, the institute confirmed.

The high number of vacancies is impacting on services on the ground in key areas, such as the schools programme, where there are just part-time two nurses to cover 93 schools across the county. “Less than two thirds of the school population will get a service this year unless we get more staff,” a nurse told the Herald.

The role of public health nurses in the area of child protection was also raised, in particular the considerable workload that such cases bring at the expense of other duties. “We are doing our very best and certainly if there is a serious situation it will be a priority but others things will have to lose out,” one nurse said.

Others noted that there had been an increase in the number of social workers in the county following recommendations in the area of child protection but that the same had not applied to public health nurses, who were joint partners in the child protection process.

The meeting also heard concerns about the need for administrative support and the impact of cuts to home help hours. Nurses said that the home help sector was “in crisis” and that it was not possible for them to plug gaps where home help hours had been cut.

Before the meeting concluded Deputy Feighan committed to organising a meeting between the Institute and the Health Minister on the public health nursing crisis in the county. It was also agreed to seek a meeting with the HSE.
