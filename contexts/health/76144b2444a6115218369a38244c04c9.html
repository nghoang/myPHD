Expressions of interest to create academic health science networks sought

21 June, 2012

Expressions of interest are sought to create an academic health science network (AHSNs) . The goal of AHSNÕs will be to improve patient and population health outcomes by translating research into practice and developing and implementing integrated health care systems. The document sets out the draft designation and establishment process.

Every local NHS organisation should aspire to be affiliated to its local AHSN, which would act as a gateway for any NHS organisation needing support or help with innovation, and provide industry with focused points of access to the NHS.

The academic health science networks guidance (PDF 229Kb) is aimed at the NHS, care trust, special health authorities, and local authority chief executives, medical, nursing, finance and public health directors,Ê NHS chairs, university deans of medical schools, schools of nursing and midwifery and allied health professionals.

Read the letter on Academic Health Science Networks (PDF, 49K) from Sir David Nicholson, NHS Chief Executive and Professor Dame Sally C Davies, Chief Medical Officer and Chief Scientific Adviser

Innovation health and wealth (IHW) is the NHS Chief ExecutiveÕs report on the adoption and spread of innovation in the NHS. Launched by the Prime Minister in December 2011 it sets out the contribution the NHS can make to the GovernmentÕs Plan for Growth. IHW recommended that the NHS Chief Executive and the Chief Medical Officer should work with partners to designate academic health science networks (AHSNs) that will align education, clinical research, informatics, training and healthcare delivery.
