FHS news and information


The Faculty of Health Sciences releases its annual report and celebrates the 2012 award recipients
On November 1, the Faculty of Health Sciences held its two major events: its Annual General Meeting and its 11th Ceremony of Excellence. The Interim Dean first presented a summary of the Faculty?s activities and financial statements, and the Faculty honoured the academic success of the Faculty's best students and the achievements of professors and staff members who have excelled during the 2011-2012 academic year.

Reproductive tourism: Paying women in poor countries to bear our babies is rife with ethical problems
Reproductive tourism, the phenomenon of people crossing international borders to purchase assisted reproductive technologies (ARTs) is now a multi-billion dollar industry. Professor Raywat Deonandan (ISHS), along with students Samantha Green and Amanda van Beinum, enumerate the specific ethical challenges posed by this emerging new industry.

The October issue of the FHS eNewsletter is now online
In this issue, Dr. Sylvie Lauzon, interim dean of the FHS, announces the new director and associate dean of the School of Human Kinetics, and invites FHS members and friends to attend the upcoming annual faculty gathering on November 1.

Two prominent FHS scientists receive Leaders Opportunity Fund
Professors Shannon Bainbridge and Roanne Thomas will receive funds from the Canada Foundation for Innovation (CFI) to support their cutting-edge research in the areas of prenatal health and chronic illnesses and disability, respectively.
