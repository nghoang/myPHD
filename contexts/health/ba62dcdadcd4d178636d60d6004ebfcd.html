New health screening for children in Abu Dhabi

Rules adopted for screenings to detect medical concerns and monitor development

  • By Samihah Zaman, Staff Reporter
  • Published: 18:12 November 6, 2012

Abu Dhabi: A set of standards to govern medical checks for healthy children up to the age of six-years-old has recently been introduced by the Health Authority Abu Dhabi (HAAD).

The standards, which will be implemented across all healthcare facilities in the emirate by the end of March 2013, include guidelines about their vaccination schedules. monitoring childrens growth and development between the age of one-week-old and six-years-old.

To ensure the healthy growth and development of a child, regular paediatric screenings are recommended. The HAAD standards on Well Child Visits therefore sets certain development milestones that must be monitored, as well as specific screenings that must be conducted, during each visit to the doctor, Dr Jennifer Moore, section head for maternal and child health at HAAD, told Gulf News.

While a number of hospitals are already stringent about regular checks for children in this age group, the introduction of the HAAD Standard on Well Child Visits is expected to standardise the monitoring of normal child growth.

For example, all hospitals will be using the same type of growth chart for children and they will screen children for autism using a set questionnaire, Dr Moore explained.

A number of paediatricians in the emirate have already been trained to follow the Well Child Visit guidelines, as well as to encourage regular doctor visits by parents of small children.

Parents need to understand the importance of regular doctor visits, and we are training paediatricians to convey this information. We also conducted a needs assessment among paediatricians, and based on this, the first training sessions for them in October focussed on detecting autism risk, monitoring normal development and managing paediatric asthma, Dr Moore said.

All newborn children in the emirate of Abu Dhabi currently undergo a series of physical examinations, and are also screened for critical congenital heart disease. Other screenings are conducted when children begin to attend school.

These Well Child Visits therefore fill the gap for regular screenings for children who fall within these two age groups, explained Moore.

The screening guidelines have been introduced under the patronage of Her Highness Shaikha Fatima Bin Mubarak, chairwoman of the Family Development Foundation and the General Womens Union.

According to 2011 statistics, over one fifth of Abu Dhabi emirates population is under the age of 19-years-old. Therefore, safeguarding the health of children is a top priority for us. Research also shows that early intervention and treatment services can greatly improve a childs development, said Zaid Al Siksek, chief executive officer at HAAD.

Emirati childrens medical visits in the emirate of Abu Dhabi are covered under the Thiqa plan, while expatriate childrens insurance coverage depends on their specific insurance plan.

In case an expatriate child aged between one-week-old and six-years-old does not have insurance coverage, parents can still avail of the Well Child Visit for their children at any public healthcare facility operated by the Abu Dhabi Health Services Company (SEHA), the HAAD statement said.
